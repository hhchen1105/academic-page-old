Helpful books/papers/notes/videos

Here I list resources (e.g., books/papers/notes) I found helpful.
  • Networks, Crowds, and Markets by David Easley and Jon Kleinberg

  • The elements of statistical learning by Trevor Hastie, Robert Tibshirani, and Jerome Friedman

  • Deep Learning book by Yoshua Bengio, Ian J. Goodfellow, and Aaron Courville

  • Deep Learning Talk 1 and Deep Learning Talk 2 + 3 by Quoc Le at MLSS 2014

  • Introduction to Information Retrieval by Christopher D. Manning, Prabhakar Raghavan, and Hinrich Schütze
    A classic IR textbook

  • Exercises in Programming Style by Cristina Lopes.
    A great talk on the same topic given by Prof. Lopes at StrangeLoop. Lopes' blog and GitHub on the book.

  • The Bridges to Fermat's Last Theorem - Numberphile by Numberphile
    Prof. Ken Ribet showed how a mathematician may tackle a problem
